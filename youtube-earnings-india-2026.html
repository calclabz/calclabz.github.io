How Much Does YouTube Pay Per 1000 Views in India in 2026?

📅 March 2026  |  ⏱️ 5 min read  |  ✍️ CalcLabz Team

If you are an Indian YouTuber or planning to start a YouTube channel, one of the most common questions you probably have is — "How much does YouTube pay per 1000 views in India?"

The answer is not straightforward — it depends on your niche, your audience location, and the type of content you create. In this guide, we break down everything you need to know about YouTube earnings in India in 2026.

💡 Quick Answer: In India, YouTube pays an average of ₹30 to ₹200 per 1000 views. This varies significantly based on your content niche, audience country, and video format.

How Are YouTube Earnings Calculated?

YouTube earnings are primarily calculated using RPM (Revenue Per Mille) — which means the revenue earned per 1000 views after YouTube takes its 45% cut.

Your RPM depends on several key factors:

✅ Content niche — Finance and Technology channels earn significantly more than Entertainment or Comedy channels.

✅ Audience location — Viewers from the USA, UK, and Canada generate much higher ad revenue compared to Indian viewers.

✅ Video type — Long-form videos earn more than YouTube Shorts.

✅ Season — RPM is highest during Q4 (October to December) due to increased advertiser spending.

YouTube Shorts vs Long Videos — Which Pays More?

YouTube Shorts earn significantly less than long-form videos. On average, Shorts earn approximately 10% of what long videos earn per 1000 views.

For example, if a long video earns ₹100 per 1000 views, a Short on the same channel will earn approximately ₹10 per 1000 views.

📊 Shorts vs Long Videos: Long-form videos generate more ad revenue, but YouTube Shorts can help grow your channel faster. The best strategy is to use both formats together.

How to Increase Your YouTube Earnings in India?

Here are some proven strategies to boost your YouTube revenue as an Indian creator:

✅ Target international audiences — Viewers from the USA, UK, and Canada generate 5–10x more ad revenue than Indian viewers.

✅ Choose high RPM niches — Finance, Technology, and Education are the top-paying niches in India.

✅ Create longer videos — Videos between 8 to 15 minutes allow more mid-roll ads, increasing your overall earnings.

✅ Upload more during Q4 — October to December is the highest RPM season due to festive advertising budgets.

✅ Diversify with sponsorships — Brand deals and sponsorships often pay more than AdSense alone.

Conclusion

YouTube earnings in India vary widely depending on your niche, audience location, and content strategy. On average, Indian creators earn between ₹30 to ₹200 per 1000 views — but with the right niche and strategy, this number can be significantly higher.

If you are serious about growing your YouTube income, focus on high-RPM niches like Finance or Technology, target international viewers, and stay consistent with your content.
