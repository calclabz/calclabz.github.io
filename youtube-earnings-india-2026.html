How Are YouTube Earnings Calculated in India?

YouTube earnings are primarily calculated using RPM (Revenue Per Mille) — which means the revenue earned per 1000 views after YouTube takes its 45% cut. This is different from CPM (Cost Per Mille) which is what advertisers pay before YouTube's cut.

Your YouTube RPM in India depends on several key factors:

✅ Content niche — Finance and Technology channels earn significantly more than Entertainment or Comedy channels.

✅ Audience location — Viewers from the USA, UK, and Canada generate much higher ad revenue compared to Indian viewers.

✅ Video type — Long-form videos earn more than YouTube Shorts.

✅ Season — RPM is highest during Q4 (October to December) due to increased advertiser spending.

YouTube Shorts vs Long Videos — Which Pays More in India?

Many Indian creators wonder about YouTube Shorts earnings in India compared to long-form videos. YouTube Shorts earn significantly less than long-form videos. On average, YouTube Shorts RPM in India is approximately 10% of what long videos earn per 1000 views.

For example, if a long video earns ₹100 per 1000 views, a Short on the same channel will earn approximately ₹10 per 1000 views. However, Shorts can help grow your channel faster and bring more subscribers.

📊 Shorts vs Long Videos: Long-form videos generate more YouTube ad revenue in India, but YouTube Shorts can help grow your channel faster. The best strategy is to use both formats together for maximum growth and earnings.

How to Increase Your YouTube Earnings in India?

Here are some proven strategies to boost your YouTube income in India as an Indian creator:

✅ Target international audiences — Viewers from the USA, UK, and Canada generate 5–10x more ad revenue than Indian viewers. This is the fastest way to increase your YouTube RPM in India.

✅ Choose high RPM niches — Finance, Technology, and Education are the top-paying niches for Indian YouTubers earnings.

✅ Create longer videos — Videos between 8 to 15 minutes allow more mid-roll ads, increasing your overall YouTube channel earnings.

✅ Upload more during Q4 — October to December is the highest YouTube CPM India season due to festive advertising budgets.

✅ Diversify with sponsorships — Brand deals and sponsorships often pay more than AdSense alone for Indian content creators.
